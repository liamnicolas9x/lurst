\# DEVELOPMENT ROADMAP



\---



\# OVERALL STRATEGY



The project must be developed in small stable phases.



Rules:

\- every phase must remain playable

\- never jump ahead

\- never build future systems early

\- prioritize stable foundations first

\- multiplayer stability is more important than features



Development priority:

1\. networking

2\. RTS controls

3\. persistence

4\. core simulation

5\. atmosphere

6\. polish



\---

Goal:

Create a lightweight hybrid-persistent multiplayer RTS foundation.



\## Features

\- project setup

\- Phaser frontend

\- Vercel backend functions
\- Supabase realtime synchronization
\- hybrid persistence foundation

\- login/account system

\- persistent database setup

\- handmade map loading

\- camera movement

\- edge scrolling

\- zoom in/out

\- minimap foundation

\- unit rendering

\- realtime multiplayer movement

\- player synchronization

\- spectator mode

\- global chat

\- day/night visuals

\- ambient lighting

\- basic ambient audio



\## Units

\- single villager only

\- no AI yet



\## Excluded

\- buildings

\- combat

\- wildlife

\- resources

\- hunger

\- pathfinding complexity



\## Goal Result

Players can:

\- login

\- move around persistent world

\- see each other

\- chat

\- spectate

\# SUCCESS TARGET



The game succeeds if:

\- the world feels alive

\- villages feel personal

\- players become emotionally attached to their settlements

\- the multiplayer world feels persistent

\- the RTS controls feel satisfying

\- the atmosphere feels cozy and memorable
